**Question 43 Model Answer:**

(a) Stages of mitosis:

1. **Prophase:** Chromosomes condense, spindle fibers form, nuclear envelope breaks down
2. **Metaphase:** Chromosomes align at the cell's equator
3. **Anaphase:** Sister chromatids separate and move to opposite poles
4. **Telophase:** Nuclear envelopes reform, chromosomes decondense

Significance: Produces two identical daughter cells for growth, repair, and asexual reproduction.

(b) Comparison:

- **Mitosis:** Produces 2 identical diploid cells, maintains chromosome number
- **Meiosis:** Produces 4 haploid cells, introduces genetic variation through crossing over and independent assortment
- **Mitosis:** For somatic cells (growth, repair)
- **Meiosis:** For gamete production (reproduction)

**Question 45 Model Answer:**

(a) An ecosystem consists of:

- **Biotic Components:** Living organisms (producers, consumers, decomposers)
- **Abiotic Components:** Physical environment (soil, water, sunlight, temperature)

(b) Energy flow:

- Energy decreases at each trophic level (10% rule)

**Question 46 Model Answer:**

(a) Natural selection:

1. **Variation:** Individuals within a population have different traits
2. **Differential Reproduction:** Organisms with beneficial traits survive and reproduce more
3. **Inheritance:** Beneficial traits pass to offspring
4. **Result:** Favorable traits increase in population frequency over generations

Example: Antibiotic resistance in bacteria

- Some bacteria have genes for resistance
- Antibiotics kill non-resistant bacteria
- Resistant bacteria survive and reproduce
- Population becomes resistant over time

(b) Evidence for evolution:

- **Fossil Record:** Shows progression of species over time
- **Comparative Anatomy:** Homologous structures in different species
- **Molecular Biology:** DNA/protein similarities among species
- **Biogeography:** Species distribution patterns

Mutations provide genetic variation:

- Random changes in DNA create new alleles
- Most mutations are neutral or harmful, but some are beneficial
- Sexual reproduction shuffles alleles, increasing variation
- Natural selection acts on this variation
