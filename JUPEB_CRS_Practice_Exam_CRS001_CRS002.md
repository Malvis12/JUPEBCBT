### **Question 41 (CRS 001 & 002): Explain the significance of the Ten Commandments in Judeo-Christian tradition (10 marks)**

**Model Answer:**

The Ten Commandments are fundamental to Judeo-Christian ethics and law. Here are the key points:

**(a) Divine Law and Covenant:**

- Given by God to Moses on Mount Sinai after the Exodus
- Represented God's covenant with the Israelite people
- Form the foundational moral and civil law for ancient Israel

**(b) Content and Categories:**

- **First four commandments**: Relate to humanity's relationship with God (worship, idolatry, Sabbath)
- **Last six commandments**: Regulate human relationships (honor parents, prohibit murder, theft, adultery, false witness, covetousness)

**(c) Theological Significance:**

- Reflect God's holiness and moral character
- Establish the basis for justice and social order
- In Christian theology, summarized by Jesus into two: love God and love your neighbor
- Demonstrate the impossibility of human perfection without divine grace

**(d) Continuing Relevance:**

- Foundation of Western legal systems
- Central to religious education in Judaism and Christianity
- Represent ethical principles that transcend time and culture
- In Christianity, fulfill the law through Christ's sacrificial death

**(e) Conclusion:**
The Ten Commandments remain central to understanding God's revelation and the moral framework of Western civilization.

### **Question 42 (CRS 001): Discuss the role of prophets in Old Testament Israel (10 marks)**

**Model Answer:**

Prophets held a crucial position in ancient Israel's religious and political life:

**(a) Definition and Function:**

- Prophets were individuals called by God to communicate His will to the people
- Served as intermediaries between God and the Israelite community
- Proclaimed God's message through prophecy, signs, and miracles

**(b) Types of Prophets:**
| Prophet | Period | Primary Message |
|---------|--------|-----------------|
| Elijah | 9th century BC | Oppose idolatry |
| Isaiah | 8th century BC | Judgment and salvation |
| Jeremiah | 7th-6th century BC | Warning of exile |
| Ezekiel | 6th century BC | Hope during exile |

**(c) Roles and Responsibilities:**

- **Spiritual leadership**: Maintained covenant relationship with God
- **Moral authority**: Condemned injustice and social sin
- **Political counsel**: Advised kings on matters of state and God's will
- **Predictive role**: Foretold future events and consequences

**(d) Methods and Validation:**

- Received God's word through visions and auditions
- Legitimacy tested by: accuracy of predictions, alignment with God's law, personal holiness
- Used symbolic actions to communicate messages (e.g., Jeremiah breaking pottery)

**(e) Impact on Israel:**

- Shaped national identity and religious practice
- Preserved moral standards during decline
- Provided hope during times of exile and distress
- Laid foundation for messianic expectations

### **Question 43 (CRS 002): Analyze the life and teachings of Jesus Christ and their impact on Christianity (10 marks)**

**Model Answer:**

Jesus Christ is the central figure of Christianity. His life and teachings fundamentally shaped the faith:

**(a) Birth, Ministry, and Core Teachings:**

- Born in Bethlehem to Mary, though Christians believe he is the Son of God
- Ministry lasted approximately 3 years (29-33 AD)
- Primary locations: Judea, Galilee, and Jerusalem
- Key teachings: Kingdom of God, redemption, love, forgiveness

**(b) Central Themes:**
| Theme | Explanation |
|-------|-------------|
| Kingdom of God | Present in Jesus' actions and future kingdom |
| Salvation | Available through faith and repentance |
| Grace | God's unmerited favor toward humanity |
| Redemption | Through Jesus' sacrificial death |

**(c) Miracles and Signs:**

- Healing the sick (leprosy, paralysis, blindness)
- Raising the dead (Lazarus, widow's son)
- Controlling nature (calming storm, feeding 5,000)
- Purpose: Demonstrated God's power and compassion

**(d) Death and Resurrection:**

- Crucifixion interpreted as atonement for human sin
- Resurrection on third day confirms power over death
- Central to Christian faith: "Christ died for our sins"
- Establishes basis for Christian hope and salvation

**(e) Impact on Christianity:**

- Fulfillment of Old Testament messianic prophecies
- Establishes church as "body of Christ"
- Death and resurrection become Christian's model for salvation
- His teachings become foundation of Christian ethics and theology

### **Question 44 (CRS 002): Explain the role of the apostles in establishing the early church (10 marks)**

**Model Answer:**

The apostles were instrumental in founding and spreading Christianity:

**(a) Definition and Selection:**

- "Apostle" means "one sent" or messenger
- Jesus chose 12 apostles (plus later additions like Paul)
- They were witnesses of Jesus' ministry, death, and resurrection
- Received authority and the Holy Spirit at Pentecost

**(b) Roles and Responsibilities:**

| Responsibility  | Description                                |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Leadership      | Guided church decisions and governance     |
| Teaching        | Transmitted Jesus' teachings and doctrines |
| Witness         | Testified to resurrection and God's grace  |
| Missionary Work | Spread Christianity beyond Jerusalem       |
| Administration  | Distributed resources and settled disputes |

**(c) Key Contributions:**

- **Peter**: Leadership role, ministry to Jews, established church authority
- **Paul**: Missionary journeys, letters/epistles, Gentile outreach
- **James**: Leadership in Jerusalem church, maintained Jewish traditions
- **John**: Theological depth, emphasized God's love

**(d) Challenges Faced:**

- Persecution from Jewish authorities
- Conflict with Roman government
- Internal theological disputes
- Need to organize growing movement
- Cultural barriers (Jewish vs. Gentile inclusion)

**(e) Legacy:**

- Established doctrinal foundations of Christianity
- Created church structures (bishops, elders, deacons)
- Wrote New Testament scriptures
- Expanded from Jerusalem to entire Mediterranean
- Their martyrdom strengthened church commitment

### **Question 45 (CRS 001 & 002): Compare and contrast Judaism and Christianity in terms of their sacred texts, beliefs, and practices (10 marks)**

**Model Answer:**

Judaism and Christianity share common roots but diverge in significant ways:

**(a) Sacred Texts:**

| Aspect         | Judaism                                | Christianity                  |
| -------------- | -------------------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Hebrew Bible   | The Tanakh (Torah, Prophets, Writings) | Old Testament + New Testament |
| Oral Tradition | Talmud (Mishnah + Gemara)              | Church Tradition and Creeds   |
| Authority      | Torah most authoritative               | Jesus Christ as Word of God   |

**(b) Understanding of God:**

- **Judaism**: Strict monotheism, God is transcendent and holy
- **Christianity**: Belief in Trinity (God as Father, Son, Holy Spirit)
- **Jesus**: Jews await Messiah; Christians believe Jesus is the Messiah and Son of God

**(c) Salvation and Redemption:**

- **Judaism**: Salvation through following Torah and God's covenant; emphasis on this-worldly restoration
- **Christianity**: Salvation through faith in Jesus Christ and his atoning death; emphasis on eternal life

**(d) Religious Practices:**

| Practice        | Judaism                               | Christianity                 |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Sabbath         | Saturday (holy day of rest)           | Sunday (day of resurrection) |
| Dietary Laws    | Strictly observed (kosher)            | Generally not observed       |
| Circumcision    | Sign of covenant                      | Not required                 |
| Sacrifice       | Ended with Temple destruction (70 AD) | Jesus as final sacrifice     |
| Prayer Emphasis | Individual and communal prayer        | Prayer to God through Jesus  |

**(e) Historical Relationship:**

- Christianity emerged from Judaism in 1st century
- Early church included Jewish and Gentile believers
- Separation accelerated after Temple destruction
- Both traditions evolved independently
- Modern interfaith dialogue recognizes common heritage

### **Question 46 (CRS 001 & 002): Discuss the concept of covenant in the Bible and its theological significance (10 marks)**

**Model Answer:**

Covenant is a central biblical concept establishing God's relationship with humanity:

**(a) Definition and Types:**

- **Covenant**: Binding agreement between God and His people, involving promises, conditions, and signs
- **Covenants in Scripture**:
  - **Noahic**: God promises not to destroy humanity; sign is rainbow
  - **Abrahamic**: God promises land and descendants; sign is circumcision
  - **Mosaic**: God gives law; condition is obedience
  - **Davidic**: God promises eternal dynasty; condition is faithfulness
  - **New Covenant**: God forgives sin through Christ; sign is blood of Christ

**(b) Structure of Covenants:**

| Component         | Purpose                               |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Divine Initiative | Shows God's sovereignty               |
| Promise           | Assures God's blessing and protection |
| Condition/Law     | Establishes human responsibility      |
| Sign/Seal         | Provides visible reminder             |
| Consequence       | Outlines blessing or curse            |

**(c) Theological Significance in Old Testament:**

- Explains God's relationship with Israel as chosen people
- Establishes basis for law and social order
- Provides hope through repeated renewal despite failures
- Foundation for understanding prophetic warnings and promises

**(d) Covenant Theology in Christianity:**

- **Old Covenant**: Law given at Sinai (Mosaic Covenant)
- **New Covenant**: Foretold by Jeremiah, fulfilled in Christ
- Jesus' death replaces animal sacrifice
- Grace replaces legalism
- Faith in Christ becomes condition for salvation
- Church understands itself as new covenant community

**(e) Continuing Relevance:**

- Explains why God maintains relationship despite human sin
- Offers theological framework for divine justice and mercy
- Provides hope for restoration and redemption
- Unites Old and New Testament narratives
- Foundation for understanding Christian salvation

## STUDY TIPS FOR CRS 001 & 002

1. **Timeline Mastery**: Create a chronological timeline of biblical events from Creation to Jesus' time. Include major figures, dates, and significance. This helps visualize the flow of history.

2. **Map Knowledge**: Familiarize yourself with biblical geography—locations of Judea, Galilee, Egypt, Babylon, Jerusalem. Understanding geographical context enhances comprehension.

3. **Prophet Profiles**: Create summary cards for major prophets (Elijah, Isaiah, Jeremiah) including their period, key message, and major acts. This aids memorization.

4. **Covenant Comparison**: Create a table comparing all five major covenants: parties involved, promises, conditions, signs, and significance. This helps understand God's progressive revelation.

5. **Gospel Perspectives**: Understand the four gospels' unique perspectives: Matthew (Jewish Jesus), Mark (servant), Luke (universal savior), John (divine Word). Note differences and similarities.

6. **Theological Concepts**: Master key terms like grace, redemption, atonement, justification, sanctification. Understand how these apply to salvation theology.

7. **Apostolic Missions**: Track Paul's missionary journeys on a map. Understand which churches he founded and issues he addressed in his epistles.

8. **Practice Debates**: Prepare to explain Christian positions on: why Jesus is God, how faith and works relate, the role of law vs. grace, Jewish-Gentile relationships in early church.
